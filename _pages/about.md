I am an evolutionary ecologist investigating how global environmental change reshapes biodiversity across genomic, species, and ecosystem levels, with a particular focus on insect pollinators. By integrating evolutionary ecology, population genomics, and spatiotemporal modelling, I aim to uncover how anthropogenic stressors—such as urban intensification and heat—drive genomic, demographic, and community-level changes in wild pollinator populations. My approach combines molecular methods (whole-genome resequencing, pollen metabarcoding) with high-resolution observational studies and spatiotemporally explicit models to better understand and anticipate biodiversity responses in the Anthropocene.
